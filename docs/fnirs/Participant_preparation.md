# Participant Preparation

## 1. Before the Cap Goes On
- Briefly introduce the fNIRS machine to the participant (and parent, for child participants); have them sit comfortably.
- **Glasses:** remove before cap placement; can be re-worn once the cap is on.
- **Long hair:** let it down first — easier to manage during optode placement.
- Materials to have ready: consent/verbal consent forms, child- and parent-friendly instructions.

---

## 2. Measure Head Circumference & Choose Cap Size
Locate the four 10-20 landmarks and wrap the tape measure along this path:

**Nz → T4 → Iz → T3 → Nz**

| Landmark | Definition |
|---|---|
| Nasion (Nz) | Between the eyebrows, at the bridge of the nose |
| Inion (Iz) | Largest projection at the back of the skull, just above where skull meets spine |
| Left/Right preauricular (LPA/RPA) | Just in front of the ear, where jaw meets skull |
| Cz | Center: equidistant from Nz/Iz **and** from LPA/RPA |

- If the measurement falls between sizes, **round down to the smaller size**.
- Parents can also be asked in advance to self-measure and report head circumference.

---

## 3. Placing the Cap
1. Pre-populate the cap with all optodes, cable trees, and Velcro straps **before** placement (see [Populating the fNIRS Cap](./Populating-the-fNIRS-Cap)).
2. Stand in front of the seated participant. Line up the front of the cap with their forehead, then pull down evenly on all sides.
3. If the montage covers haired regions, sweep the cap up/down and side-to-side (or have the participant "shimmy" their head) so optode tips comb through the hair.
4. Check that:
   - The cap sits evenly, no bunched fabric
   - Both ears fit comfortably through the ear holes
   - The seam sits **above the eyebrows** and is level (not tilted)
   - The cap tag (back of cap) is pulled out
5. Verify landmark spacing:
   - **Fpz is equidistant** between Nz and AFz
   - **Cz is equidistant** from Nz/Iz and from LPA/RPA
6. Gently close the chin strap — snug, not tight.

> **For analysis accuracy:** A digitizer (3D camera/Polhemus) gives the most accurate optode position record. If unavailable, record head circumference, Nz–Iz, and LPA–RPA distances per participant for later registration.

---

## 4. Environmental Light Control
Ambient/IR light can interfere with signal quality (raises dark noise). Options:
- Run the session in a relatively dark room (may not suit younger children).
- Use blackout clothing/an overcap over the fNIRS cap after it's placed.
- Overcap size: roughly **4 cm larger** than the participant's head circumference.

---

## 5. Quick Pre-Recording Checklist
- [ ] Glasses removed
- [ ] Long hair let down
- [ ] Cap seam level, above eyebrows
- [ ] Fpz/Cz landmark spacing verified
- [ ] Chin strap snug, comfortable
- [ ] Cap tag pulled out
- [ ] Light control in place (room/overcap) if needed
